# Design — Research: Programme Boards with Schools · v6

Stellar Syndicates · **architecture decided: Hybrid** — Programme Boards, verb-gated tiers, continuous goods-funded clock. **v6: exclusivity removed.** Every programme in every school is researchable by every corp; differentiation comes from *sequencing* on the continuous clock, verb gates, and season length. Six fields, twelve schools. Reverse engineering and tech trading remain deferred.

## Structure

Every field: **Tier I (open to all) → Tier II (field verb gate) → two Schools, each with its own verb gate and Tiers III–V.**

- **Nothing is exclusive.** Both schools of a field can be pursued; each opens on its own school-flavored verb gate, so your biography still decides which door opens first.
- **Ladder rule**: Tier N+1 of a ladder opens when *any one* Tier-N programme on that ladder completes (plus the verb gate). Rushing deep means skipping siblings — you can return for them, later, at the cost of everything else you aren't researching.
- ~108 authored programmes on a one-at-a-time continuous clock: a season yields roughly a third of the tree. Identity is the *order you chose*; it fades only as a season ends, and seasons end.

## Board discipline (balance laws)

1. Within a tier, programmes are sidegrades: no research *ordering* is strictly dominant, and every programme is a defensible first pick for some strategy.
2. Percentages live in Tiers I–III; **Tiers IV–V pay primarily in capabilities.**
3. Exactly one fork per field. **New hulls live only in the Hulls field — two in the whole tree**: Destroyer (Line IV) and Cruiser (Line V). Everything else that changes a ship is a module or a refit variant, never a new ShipKind.
4. Nothing violates lightspeed; entangled keys carry no information.
5. School gates are school-flavored verbs; Tier I never gates; all thresholds/baskets `Tunable`, sized to season length.
6. Deferred-scope prizes populate deep tiers: ECM-as-sensor-warfare, utility modules, experts, tier-IV/V structures, salvage, capture-in-transit.
7. The counter matrix stays one-to-one: no research may let a weapon ignore its counter or double-counter a weapon.
8. **Pacing is the balance surface.** With no exclusivity, concurrency and programme durations are the only anti-convergence knobs: one concurrent programme corp-wide, and a duration curve steep enough that a focused corp finishes ~35% of the tree per season.

## Funding & pacing

Basket escalation unchanged (Electronics-light → Rare-Elements-heavy, field-flavored: Weapons wants Armaments, Life wants Biomass/Provisions, Hulls wants Machinery). Pacing targets: Tier I programmes land in week one; school gates open mid-season as biographies accumulate; a focused corp reaches **one or two Tier Vs** and ~35% total coverage by season's end. Duration curve: deep tiers run long (T1 hours-to-a-day, T5 a week-plus) so late convergence never happens inside a season. No two corps end a season with the same order — and at a third coverage, rarely the same sheet.

**Future hook (not v1)**: cross-field synthesis programmes requiring picks in two schools (Fleet Tenders + Watch III → mobile sensor pickets). Parked.

## Remaining open questions (exclusivity: RESOLVED — none)

1. **Hosting** — Academy dual-duty (lean) vs. dedicated Institute.
2. **Concurrency** — now the primary anti-convergence knob. One programme corp-wide (lean: firm) · 2-concurrent ceiling · per Academy system (not recommended without exclusivity — it converges everyone).
3. **Funding** — up-front debit vs. continuous drip (lean: drip).
4. **Rate** — fixed duration vs. throughput-scaled by Academy × affine specialists × food state (lean: throughput-scaled).
5. **Effect application** — instant galaxy-wide (lean for v1) vs. propagate-at-c (the purist upgrade — your call wanted).

# Claude Code Handoff — Research: Programme Boards with Schools
Repo: `peterholko/stellar-syndicates` · sim + server view + client. Implements the v6 research design (six fields, twelve schools, ~108 programmes, **no exclusivity**). Land as compiling, test-green phases R1→R6.
## Locked decisions
Hybrid Programme Boards, verb-gated tiers, **no exclusivity** (differentiation = sequencing) · **Hosting**: Academy dual-duty (training + research) · **Concurrency**: ONE active programme per syndicate; **every staffed Academy adds research rate (Civ-style, additive, distributed)** · **Funding**: continuous drip — each contributing Academy drips its share of the basket from its *own* system stockpile · **Rate**: throughput-scaled (Academy tier curve × staffing × affine specialists × food state) · **Effects**: apply **instantly galaxy-wide** on completion · Ladder rule: Tier N+1 opens when any one Tier-N programme on that ladder completes, plus the tier's verb gate; Tier I never gates · Tech sheets are **private** in v1 (no rival research intel UI; battle records already leak fielded tech organically) · Reverse engineering, tech trading, and the *implementations* of Salvage Rigs / Boarding Parties are deferred — those two catalog entries ship `hidden: true`.
## Design laws
1. **Determinism & compat**: pure seeded sim; `BTreeMap`/`BTreeSet` for all keyed state; catalog is `const`; every new field `#[serde(default)]` — old snapshots load with empty research and tick clean, no migration.
2. **Shown math**: the active programme's rate is a visible sum of per-Academy factor chains (tier throughput × staffing × skill × food); gate progress is a visible counter.
3. **Fog**: a syndicate's research state (active, queue, progress, verbs, completed) is owner-only in the view. Nothing new leaks.
4. **Lightspeed**: verb counters increment when the *sim* resolves the event (the sim is the ledger of what happened, not what's been seen); effects apply instantly by decision #5 — document this as the one deliberate purism concession.
5. **Matrix integrity**: no effect may let a weapon ignore its counter or double-counter a weapon (law 7 of the design doc). `Flak Doctrine`/`Ablative`/`Spall` tune existing counter constants only.
6. **Pacing is the balance surface**: tier costs form a steep curve (defaults below, all `Tunable`) targeting ~35% tree coverage per season for a focused syndicate.

## Part R2 — The distributed clock
Research is a production job, not a new machine. Add `JobKind::Research` to production assignments, valid only on bodies with an Academy:
```
rate_i = academy_tier_throughput(tier)        // the existing 1.0/2.2/3.8/6.0 curve
       × staffing(workers/required)
       × skill(affine specialists, 1.75)      // affinity table below
       × food_state_factor(system)
drip_i = basket_per_rate_second(field, tier) × rate_i     // from THIS system's stockpile
```
An Academy whose stockpile can't cover its drip this tick **suspends its contribution** (soft, resumes automatically) — a blockaded lab slows the syndicate's science, visibly. Total: `progress += Σ rate_i × dt` over contributing Academies. No Academies staffed → `Event::ResearchStalled` (once, with resume event).
Field affinity (`Tunable`): Propulsion → PetrochemicalEngineer · Materials → Geologist, IndustrialEngineer · Computation → IndustrialEngineer · Weapons → NavalArchitect, IndustrialEngineer · Hulls → NavalArchitect · Life → Xenobiologist.

## Tests
1. Determinism (same seed + script → identical research state hash) and serde round-trip; old snapshot → empty research, ticks clean.
2. Gate kinds: Cumulative, State, Sustained (WellSupplied 5×7d: interruption resets), ladder rule (deep-rush skips siblings; sibling researchable later).
3. Distributed clock: two staffed Academies ≈ 2× one (modulo tiers); blockading one system's inputs drops rate by exactly its contribution; drip debits local stockpiles; stall/resume events fire once.
4. Effects: spot-check one Mod per family applies at its site and only for the owning syndicate; unlock gating (Lance/Destroyer/Cruiser/T-IV builds rejected pre-research, accepted post); instant application (completion mid-battle affects next tick).
5. Matrix integrity: with all Weapons research completed, each weapon still has exactly one counter (assert the coefficient table shape).
6. Verb hooks: each verb increments from its Part-R3 site exactly once per event (raid ≠ battle double-count; RivalFleetsObserved dedupes).
7. Pacing smoke: at reference throughput, T1≈2h … T5≈168h; a scripted "focused season" completes ~35% of visible catalog.
